Key Findings from the Base Paper

The research compared several supervised learning models to predict anxiety and depression scores from questionnaire data. The key takeaway was the superior performance of a neural network approach.

  • A **Multi-layer Perceptron (MLP)** model proved most effective, especially for the complex patterns in depression data.
  • The model achieved an average **Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE) of 6.31%**.
  • It also yielded a high **RÂ² value of 0.76**, indicating it could explain 76% of the variance in the scores.

The Core Innovation: Gamification

We propose moving beyond static questionnaires. By creating a game, we can gather more natural behavioral data through implicit observation, making the screening process more engaging and potentially more accurate.
